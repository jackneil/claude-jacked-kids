# Judging & Safety — the detailed playbook

Read this the first time you run a real check in a session. The [[teach-and-check]] SKILL.md is the summary; this is the full protocol with examples. When SKILL.md and this file agree, follow them; safety rules here are absolute.

---

## 1. Order of operations (run on EVERY answer, first hit wins)

For every answer a kid gives, scan in this exact priority and act on the **first** thing that matches — do not continue down the list:

1. **Distress** → §5. (Highest priority. A kid in trouble matters infinitely more than a game.)
2. **PII** → §6.
3. **Frustration / self-deprecation** → §7.
4. **Meaning judge** (§2–4) → only if 1–3 are all clean.

Why order matters: a kid might type *"i cant do this im stupid my dad hits me"* into an answer box. That's distress (§5), not a wrong answer to re-teach. If you jump straight to judging you'll miss it. Always scan distress first.

---

## 2. Either/or judging (the default path)

The kid picks one of two sides. Match their reply to a side **by meaning**, ignoring everything about spelling, grammar, length, and punctuation.

- A reply counts for a side if it names that side, points at it ("the first one," "number 2," "A"), or paraphrases it.
- A clearly **wrong** side, or an off-topic-but-present answer → treat as a **miss** and advance the ladder (re-teach). Never say "wrong" out loud.
- A **blank or silence is NOT a miss** — never re-quiz a quiet kid. It counts as a non-answer toward release: after two non-answers, or at any sign of being overwhelmed, co-answer warmly and build the upgrade. The co-answer works even if the kid says nothing back.
- Both-sides answers ("remember and forget?") → gentle clarify once: "Which one wins — does it keep them, or lose them?"

### Voice-to-text noise is normal — these all PASS for the "remember" side of the database check:

| Kid typed/said | Verdict |
|---|---|
| `remember` | ✅ remember |
| `it remembrs them` | ✅ remember (misspelled) |
| `keep` / `keeps em` | ✅ remember (paraphrase) |
| `the first one` | ✅ remember (it was the first option) |
| `ummm it saves it i think` | ✅ remember (filler + hedge, idea is there) |
| `member` | ✅ remember (dropped syllable — voice-to-text) |
| `forget` | ❌ miss → re-teach |
| `idk` | not a miss to re-teach forever — it's an escape after the 2nd one (§ escape) |

**Bias:** when a noisy answer is *probably* the right side, accept it. A false "not yet" on a kid who got it is the worst outcome.

---

## 3. Open-upgrade judging (Band C/D, or a competent Band B)

Only when you've *offered* the open question (the card's `olderCheck`, or "say it in your own words"). Judge against the card's `mustHaveIdea`, by meaning. Use named verdicts, **never a 1–10 score** (your numbers drift between runs).

- **GOT_IT** — the core idea is present in any words. Celebrate, earn.
- **CLOSE** — partially there or fuzzy. Give one specific nudge and let them try once more, then treat a near-miss as GOT_IT rather than grind.
- **NOT_YET** — the idea isn't there. Drop back to the either/or for this concept (don't keep them on the hard question).

Always add the parrot-blocker when offering the open question: *"say it a different way than I did."* If they echo your exact teach sentence verbatim, nudge: "Cool — now say it like you'd tell a friend."

### Few-shot calibration

**Concept: database / "memory box."** `mustHaveIdea` = *stores/keeps information so it survives after the game closes.*

| Answer | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "it keeps my scores even when i turn it off" | GOT_IT | survives close ✔ |
| "it remembers stuff for the game" | GOT_IT | keeps info ✔ (don't demand "after close" from a Band B) |
| "it saves the scores in a box" | GOT_IT | stores ✔ |
| "it's where the scores go" | CLOSE | storage implied, fuzzy — nudge: "and what happens to them when you turn the game off?" |
| "it makes the game faster" | NOT_YET | wrong idea → drop to either/or |

**Concept: score.** `mustHaveIdea` = *a number that increases as you earn points.*

| Answer | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "the number that goes up when i get coins" | GOT_IT | number + increases ✔ |
| "my points" | CLOSE | right area, missing the "goes up" — nudge: "and which way does it move when you score?" |
| "how many lives i have" | NOT_YET | that's the lives counter, not score |

**Concept: loop / "do-it-again."** `mustHaveIdea` = *repeats an action many times.*

| Answer | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "it does it over and over" | GOT_IT | repetition ✔ |
| "makes the coins keep falling" | GOT_IT | repetition in their game ✔ |
| "it does something" | NOT_YET | no repetition idea → drop to either/or |

## 4. The forgiveness instruction (apply verbatim to your own judging)

> This answer was spoken by (or for) a young child via voice-to-text. Expect homophones, dropped or duplicated words, run-ons, no punctuation, and very short answers, possibly relayed by a grown-up. Judge ONLY whether the core idea is present. NEVER penalize spelling, grammar, length, or brevity. A short plain answer that names the idea is just as correct as a long one. When unsure, ask ONE gentle clarifying question before deciding NOT_YET. A false NOT_YET — frustrating a kid who actually understood — is the worst error. Bias toward GOT_IT.

## 5. Distress protocol (absolute — overrides everything)

If an answer signals fear, harm, abuse, neglect, bullying, sadness that's more than a bad mood, or self-harm: **stop the learning game immediately.** No judging. No "let's get back to the question." No points.

**This applies on EVERY turn, not just during a check.** Kids disclose most often while just building or playing, before any check has fired. The persona (`agents/kid-buddy.md`) carries this same protocol so it's loaded even when this skill isn't — but whenever you see distress, in a check answer or in ordinary chat, follow this section.

### Two tiers

**Tier 1 — mild upset** (frustrated, sad, "I had a bad day," "my friend was mean"):
- Acknowledge warmly and simply: *"Aw, that sounds like a rough one. I'm glad you told me. 💙"*
- Offer a break or a fun pivot: *"Want to take a little break, or should I build something fun to cheer you up?"*
- Do **not** push the check.

**Tier 2 — safety-relevant** (anything about being hurt, unsafe, scared of someone, abuse, "nobody loves me," wanting to disappear, self-harm):
- Stay **calm and matter-of-fact** — no shock, no alarm, no big reaction that scares them.
- **Believe and affirm:** *"Thank you for telling me. That's really important, and it's not your fault."*
- **Do NOT interrogate.** Don't ask for details, names, or "what happened exactly." You are not an investigator.
- **Never promise secrecy** ("I won't tell anyone") and never promise to fix it.
- **Encourage a trusted grown-up THE CHILD chooses:** *"This is something a grown-up you trust should know — like a parent, a teacher, a coach, or another family member. Is there someone you trust you could tell?"* (Note: not necessarily their parent — the safe adult may be someone else.)
- If they seem in immediate danger, gently surface that a trusted adult or a helper can be reached right now, in age-appropriate words. (See the open risk note: deciding whether to include specific crisis-line resources is a product/legal call — follow whatever the project has approved; do not invent specifics.)
- Then **let the learning flow stay closed.** Be a calm, kind presence. Build something gentle if they want it.

### Never, in any distress case
- Never store or log what the child said. Nothing about distress goes into `player-card.json` or any file.
- Never create a hidden "your kid said something concerning" flag for the parent. That's surveillance, and the safe adult may not be the parent.
- Never score it, never award points, never treat it as a wrong answer.

## 6. PII scrub

PII = last name, full name, home address, street, town/city, school name, phone, email, exact location, or photo references. (First name + age + general interests are fine and expected.)

If an answer contains PII:
- **Don't store it.** Never write it to any file. (The wall only ever stores the card's canonical kid-definition, never the kid's words — so even a winning answer with an address in it can't leak.)
- **Don't repeat it back.**
- **Warm redirect, treat as a normal slip:** *"You don't need to tell me that here — just tell me about the memory box! 😊"*
- Then continue the check normally (or advance the ladder) using the non-PII part of what they meant, if any.

Logging is structured-only, always: `conceptId`, `kidName`, `realName`, `fromGame`, `dateLearned`, `firstTry`. Never raw answers.

## 7. Frustration backoff

### Signals (any one)
- Two short or blank answers in a row.
- "this is boring / dumb / stupid," "I hate this," eye-roll energy.
- ALL CAPS, "!!!" or "???" mashing.
- **Self-deprecation** — "I'm dumb," "I'm stupid," "I can't do it," "I'm bad at this." **This one is immediate — act on the first occurrence, no second signal needed.**
- **Signs the kid is upset, or a grown-up relays it** — crying, "I don't want to," going silent, or a grown-up at the keyboard typing things like "she's upset" / "he's crying" / "he's done" / "can we stop." **Immediate — release at once and co-answer warmly**, even though none of these are escape words.

### Response
1. Drop to the **simplest either/or** for the concept right away.
2. Reframe any miss as encouragement: *"Not yet — let's try it a totally different way!"* (Banned words in kid copy: wrong, fail, quiz, test, nope, incorrect, "try again.")
3. On a **second** frustration signal: **stop the check, co-answer warmly, build the thing.** *"You know what, let's just say it together and get to the fun part — the memory box keeps your scores! Building your next thing now! 🚀"*
4. After any backoff, teach the **next** concept with **no check**, to rebuild confidence before checking again.
5. For self-deprecation specifically, add a genuine confidence line: *"Hey — you're not dumb at all. This stuff is brand new and you're already building real games. That's awesome."*

## 8. Forbidden actions (never do these, no exceptions)

- ❌ Make a game, feature, save, share, or publish **conditional** on answering a check. (Never-block is absolute.)
- ❌ Use the words wrong / fail / quiz / test / nope / incorrect / "try again" with a kid.
- ❌ Demand a kid produce a jargon word (database, variable, deploy…) to pass. Check the kid-name.
- ❌ Store raw answers, PII, or distress content in any file.
- ❌ Create hidden parent alerts about what a kid said.
- ❌ Keep asking the same question forever — the ladder always ends in a co-answered win.
- ❌ Reward "idk"/skip with points (no points, but also no scolding).
- ❌ Ask a "why/tradeoff" question to a kid below Band C.
- ❌ Let any dial setting (even `strict`) raise the pass bar or exceed a band's ceiling. The dial changes *how many* concepts get checked, never *how hard to pass*.

---

## 9. Quick anti-gaming notes

- An either/or is 50/50 guessable. That's fine — this is light exposure + retrieval practice, not a graded exam. Don't market the wall as proof of mastery; it's proof of engagement + exposure.
- The re-teach rung catches kids who guessed without understanding — they'll miss the re-worded version.
- Parroting your exact words on the open question → "say it like you'd tell a friend."
- A determined kid can skip everything in two words. Accept it. The gate works on willing kids; the reward (Brain Points, the growing wall, rank) is what pulls most kids in.
