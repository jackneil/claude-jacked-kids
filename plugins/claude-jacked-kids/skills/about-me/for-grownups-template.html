Your kid is building real games with a friendly buddy. While it builds, the buddy also teaches a little and checks that they got it — so they're learning, not just watching. Here's how that works and how you control it.

What "learning checks" do

When the buddy adds something new to a game (say, a way to remember high scores), it explains the idea in one kid-sized sentence, then asks a simple question — usually an either/or like "Does the memory box remember your scores, or forget them?" — before it builds the next thing your kid asks for.

It's gentle by design:

  • The game your kid already has is never taken away or blocked — a check only ever sits in front of the next new addition, and your kid gets that addition either way (answer it, or skip — they still get it).
  • If they get it wrong, the buddy explains a different way, gives a hint, and finally says the answer together — so they're never stuck.
  • They can say "skip" or "just build it" anytime, with zero penalty.
  • Getting it right earns 🧠 Brain Points and a sticker on their "Things I Learned" wall — kept totally separate from in-game points.

Honest note: this setting is saved in a plain text file on this computer (.jacked-kids/player-card.json). A tech-savvy older child could open and edit it. This is a speed bump, not a lock — it stops casual changes, not a determined kid.

Privacy & safety

  • Everything stays on this computer. Nothing about your child is sent anywhere or put online by this learning system.
  • We store almost nothing — your child's first name, age, a few interests, which ideas they've learned, and a streak. Never a last name, address, school, phone, photos, or anything they type in an answer.
  • If your child types something upsetting in an answer, the buddy stops the learning game, stays calm and kind, and encourages them to talk to a grown-up they trust. It does not record what they said, and it does not create any hidden "alert" for you — we don't surveil kids.
  • To erase all of it: delete the .jacked-kids folder in your child's game folder. That's everything.

The buddy can't do high-stakes things on its own — putting a game on the internet, spending money, or making new accounts all need a grown-up. For anything serious your child might share with the buddy, please be their safe person.
