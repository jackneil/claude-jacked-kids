Goal & locked decisions

Make learning a built-in part of how a kid uses the buddy: as the buddy builds the kid's game, every genuinely-new idea becomes a tiny Brain Boost — the buddy teaches it in one kid-sized bite tied to what's on screen, then asks a simple question the kid must engage with to unlock the next thing they want. It's a carrot taped to the side of the fun, not a fence in front of it.

Locked product decisions (do not relitigate):
  • Strictness: ON by default at normal; parent dial with four levels — off / light / normal / strict.
  • What's gated: every genuinely-new concept gets one short teach + one check, tied to the current build step.
  • Rewards: a separate "Brain Points" economy + a "Things I Learned" wall, kept distinct from in-game coins/XP.
  • Teeth: gate the next upgrade. The kid keeps everything already built & playable; the check is the toll for the next new addition. Never blocks, removes, or stalls what exists.
  • Answer channel: the kid answers in Claude Code, usually by voice-to-text (OS dictation into the CLI). No quiz UI is rendered inside the games.
The honest tension, resolved. "Semi-refuse until they answer correctly" and "never hard-block a child" can't both be absolute. Never-block wins (it's the safety floor and a persona requirement). So the gate has real teeth — it's the framed, expected path to the next thing the kid wants, and the never-stuck ladder always ends in a win — but a kid who actively refuses or melts down is waved through (taught, no Brain Points). The gate can never make the game itself conditional on an answer.
Refined after adversarial review (2026-06-27). A multi-lens DCR pass hardened the build beyond this spec's first draft. Net changes, now reflected in the shipped skills: (1) silence/overwhelm always releases — a quiet kid is never re-quizzed, and the co-answer step builds even with no reply; (2) reward is engagement-based — a kid who genuinely struggled and co-answered still earns, while "just tell me"/"idk" earns nothing; (3) the distress protocol is hoisted into the always-on persona so it covers every turn, not just check answers; (4) about-me is the sole writer of the Player Card, verifies every wall write, and creates the card on a kid's first earn; (5) strict reinforces an old concept on a separate seam, never stacking two checks; (6) the exemption is the first game per conversation. The shipped teach-and-check and about-me skills are the operative source of truth.

Interaction model (read first — it's load-bearing)

  • It's a text chat. The games render in a browser; the kid talks to the buddy through the Claude Code command line.
  • The kid answers by voice. Per the product owner, the primary channel is OS voice-to-text dictation into the CLI. Answers therefore arrive as text with voice-transcription noise — homophones, dropped words, run-ons, no punctuation, very short. We forgive all of it and judge by meaning.
  • Very young / non-readers: a grown-up or older sibling is often at the keyboard and can read the question and relay the spoken answer. If a child genuinely can't engage, the never-block floor means they simply keep playing — the gate never traps them. We do not render in-game quiz buttons (explicitly out of scope per the product owner).
  • Default check shape: an either/or question ("Does the memory box remember your stars or forget them?"). It's persona-compliant, works read aloud or spoken, and can't be defeated by spelling. An open "say it in your own words" question is an optional upgrade for older / clearly-competent kids only.

How the gate works — the flow + the ladder

When a check fires: at the seam, gating the next upgrade

Checks never interrupt a build mid-stream (a 6-year-old won't sit waiting on a half-built truck). The buddy builds the whole thing, tests it, shows it, lets the kid play. A check fires at the seam — when the kid asks for the next new thing that introduces a new gateable concept:

"YES, a laser cannon! 😎 Quick though — to earn this one you gotta tell me one thing first… [teach + check] … Nailed it! 🎉 +10 Brain Points. Building your laser cannon now! 🔧"

During the build itself the buddy still narrates ideas as one-sentence nuggets (no question, no waiting). The check is the toll for the next increment, not for anything already delivered.

The five-rung "no answer left hanging" ladder

  1. TEACH — one concrete sentence tied to their game, under the kid-name. "A memory box keeps your high scores safe even after you turn the game off."
  2. CHECK — one either/or question. Right → celebrate + Brain Points + wall sticker, done.
  3. RE-TEACH a different way (only if missed) — a new analogy, never the same words; offer a choice for autonomy ("want the toy-box version or the LEGO version?").
  4. HINT (still missed) — the smallest nudge: a sentence stem or "it KEEPS your stuff — so keep or lose?"
  5. ANSWER IT TOGETHER (the guaranteed win) — "Say it with me: the memory box KEEPS my scores! Boom. 💪" Any echo or "yeah" counts — and if the kid says nothing at all, you still say it, build the upgrade, and move on. Silence is always a release, never a loop.
Never a sixth rung of nothing. The upgrade gets built either way. The gate always opens.

Reward only follows genuine engagement

Reward follows genuine engagement, not correctness. A kid earns the sticker + Brain Points if they (a) picked the right side themselves, OR (b) engaged honestly — gave real tries on the earlier rungs — and then needed the answer said together. A kid who did not engage — escaped ("just build it" / "skip" / "boring"), gave two non-answers ("idk idk"), begged "just tell me" without trying, or only parroted — gets the upgrade built warmly but no sticker, no points (marked taught, not yet earned, may be re-offered). Skipping or begging is painless but never paid, which kills the "idk / 'just tell me' → free points" exploit while still rewarding a kid who genuinely struggled.

Escape hatches (any one ends the current check, builds anyway, no penalty)

  • Escape words: skip, stop, next, just build it, no, boring, I'm done, play, just tell me, tell me the answer, tell me.
  • Two non-answers in a row (blank, ?, idk, emoji-only).
  • Frustration / self-deprecation signals (see Safety).
  • Dial = off.

What counts as a concept worth gating

The 4-test gate (all four must be true)

  1. New to this kid — not already on their wall, not already checked this conversation.
  2. Load-bearing right now — it powers something the kid can SEE in their game.
  3. Nameable in one kid-word (memory box, loop, score).
  4. Explainable in one concrete sentence at the kid's level.

Fails any test → teach as a nugget, no check. Never gate cosmetic choices (truck color), tool mechanics, trivia, or anything already learned. On a reappearance of a learned concept, name it as a reward instead: "Another loop — you already KNOW these, training wheels off! 🚲💨"

Jargon rule: teach/check the kid-name, never demand the jargon

Per persona line 13, the kid is never required to produce a tech word to pass. Each concept has a kidName (taught & checked) and a realName (mentioned as a fun bonus, never required). "I added a memory box — grown-ups call that a 'database.' Does it remember or forget your scores?" ✅   "What is a database?" ❌ (open + jargon-first, both banned for young kids; fine to use the real term for ages 11+).

Catalog is extensible — add a card whenever a new reusable, nameable concept recurs.

Clustering & rhythm

  • Max 1 check per upgrade seam; if one request introduces several new ideas, check only the single most load-bearing and teach the rest as nuggets.
  • First game in a conversation: no check — let the kid just see a game appear. Gating starts when they ask for the next new thing that sitting. (If you can't tell whether you've already built something this conversation, skip the check.)
  • Never check a concept twice — name it as a "you already know this!" reward instead.

Age + competence difficulty model

Two axes, but only one is persisted (to dodge LLM counter-drift):

  • AGE is persisted (kid.age → derived ageBand). Sets vocabulary, concreteness, answer-length expectation, and whether the open upgrade is offered. The only persisted difficulty axis.
  • COMPETENCE is handled live, in-conversation, not persisted. Within the current chat the buddy nudges difficulty from how the last couple checks went ("nailed two — I'll offer the open version"). Qualitative in-context judgment, which the model does well. No stored offset, rolling window, or counter.

Hard clamp: never ask a "why/tradeoff" question below Band C — it just manufactures failure. No dial setting, including strict, can exceed a band's ceiling. A parent cannot dial a 6-year-old into abstraction their brain can't do yet.

Unknown age: behave at the gentlest level (either/or, concrete, short — no harder than Band A; you can't over-frustrate there), and the about-me skill warmly asks the kid's age early to calibrate. Tone stays neutral, never babyish, so an unknown-age 11-year-old isn't condescended to.

Reward framing scales with age: young kids get emoji stickers + "Brain Points" + kid-names; older kids (C/D) get a skills list using real terms ("Databases ✓, Loops ✓, Collision detection ✓") framed as a portfolio of things they actually know.

Answer-judging rubric

Either/or (the default path)

The kid picks a side. Match by meaning, not spelling: "remember," "remembr," "it remembrs them," "the first one," "keep" all map to the remember option. Voice-transcription noise (homophones, dropped words, run-ons, one-word answers) never counts against them. Ambiguous/off-topic → treat as a miss, advance the ladder (re-teach), never say "wrong."

Open upgrade (Band C/D, or competent B) — named verdicts, never a score

Judge with GOT_IT / CLOSE / NOT_YET after a one-line reason, matching against the card's mustHaveIdea by meaning. No 1–10 scale (LLM numbers drift). Synonyms and partials count.

Forgiveness instruction (verbatim in the skill): "This answer was spoken by (or for) a young child via voice-to-text. Expect homophones, dropped/duplicated words, run-ons, no punctuation, and very short answers. Judge ONLY whether the core idea is present. NEVER penalize spelling, grammar, length, or brevity. When unsure, do ONE gentle clarifying re-ask before deciding NOT_YET. A false NOT_YET (frustrating a kid who understood) is the worst error — bias toward GOT_IT."

Anti-gaming, honestly bounded

  • idk / escape → completes the teach, earns nothing. Exploit defused by removing the reward, not by punishing.
  • Parroting → the either/or can't be parroted into a correct pick without at least a 50/50 guess; the re-teach catches non-understanding. For the open upgrade, add "say it a different way than I did."
  • Honest caveat: a determined kid can guess an either/or 50/50. Acceptable — this is light exposure + retrieval, not high-stakes assessment. Don't claim the judge "ensures" comprehension.

Brain Points economy (simple, LLM-feasible)

The whole economy in three rules — no running counters the model would drift on:

  1. One wall sticker per concept genuinely learned (engaged + got it). Append-only. Never removed, never decays.
  2. Brain Points = (number of wall stickers) × 10, computed at render time. Never accumulated turn-by-turn. "130 Brain Points" just means 13 stickers, recomputed whenever shown.
  3. Rank = derived from sticker count at render time.

The "Things I Learned" wall stores only structured fields: { conceptId, kidName, realName, fromGame, dateLearned, firstTry }. It renders the canonical kid-definition from the concept card, never the kid's typed words — which removes the entire PII-leak class. The wall only ever grows.

Streak — one date field, updated once per session at start: lastDate == yesterday → current++; == today → unchanged; older → current = 1 with a warm zero-shame welcome-back (best preserved forever). A miss never breaks the streak — it counts showing up, not correctness.

Cut for v1 (YAGNI): competence state machines, exact-BP accumulation, a second rank ladder, family badges, growth-chips, combos, Brain Freezes, a cosmetic shop, spaced-callback counters. None survive a forgetful LLM cleanly and none are needed. Brain Points never buy in-game power — there's nothing to buy; the reward is the visible record of what the kid knows. No leaderboards, no peer comparison, ever.

The parent dial

Four levels change how much is checked, never how hard to pass (the lenient judge can't reliably deliver a stricter bar). All four use the same never-block floor and the same band ceiling clamp.

LevelWhat gets checkedRe-teach depthOpen upgrade?
offnothing — teach nuggets only, auto-continue. Wall doesn't grow.—no
lightonly the biggest concept, occasionally1 re-teach → co-answerno
normal defaultevery new gateable concept at its seam (1 per upgrade)full ladderonly to clearly-competent C/D kids
strictthe most load-bearing new concept — plus, on a separate later seam, a no-stakes "still got it?" reinforcement of one already-learned concept (never two checks on one seam, never a new sticker, never a gate)full ladder + one extra re-teach beatyes, to B/C/D

Who can change it: a grown-up via chat ("I'm the grown-up — set learning checks to light") writes grownups.strictness. A kid can switch checks off for the current chat only; changing the persistent default needs a grown-up to confirm (same speed-bump pattern as the publish gate). Honest limitation (stated in the explainer): the setting is a plain text file on the family's own device; a tech-savvy older child could edit it. We do not claim a real lock — the threat model is a child casually re-tuning their own learning, not an attacker.

Grown-up explainer: <arcade>/.jacked-kids/for-grownups.html, generated by the about-me skill from a template shipped inside the plugin (not from any per-machine personal template). One screen: what teach-and-check is, the dial table, how to change it, the safety posture, what's stored vs not, "erase all learning data = delete the .jacked-kids/ folder," and the honest plain-text-file note.

Safety rails

Never-block (structural)

Skill rule, verbatim: "Un-passed concepts NEVER block play, save, share, or publish. The wall is a brag sheet, never a key. A kid with zero stickers can build, play, and (with a grown-up) publish everything. No dial value, including strict, can block, delay, or revoke a game or feature. If you ever make a game conditional on an answer, you've violated the core rule — stop and just build it."

Safety order-of-operations on every free-text answer (first hit wins)

  1. Distress scan (highest priority).
  2. PII scan.
  3. Frustration / escape scan.
  4. Meaning judge — only if 1–3 are clean.

Upsetting input

If an answer signals fear/harm/abuse/self-harm: drop the gate entirely, no judging, no points. Stay calm (no shock), believe and affirm, don't interrogate, never promise secrecy, route to a trusted grown-up the child chooses, store nothing, never create a hidden "your kid said something scary" parent flag. Two tiers: mild upset → warm acknowledgment + offer a break; safety-relevant → calm affirmation + trusted-adult handoff, then stop the learning flow. See Open Risks for the limit of this posture.

Frustration backoff

Signals: two short/blank answers, "this is boring/dumb," all-caps, repeated punctuation, and self-deprecation ("I'm dumb," "I can't" — immediate, no second sign needed). Response: drop to the simplest either/or, reframe any miss as "Not yet — let's try it another way!" (the words wrong, fail, quiz, test, nope, incorrect, "try again" are banned in kid copy), and on a second signal stop the check, co-answer warmly, build the thing. After any backoff, the next concept is taught with no check to rebuild confidence.

Identity / COPPA in free-text

If an answer contains PII (last name, address, town, school, phone, email, full name, precise location, photo refs): do not store it, do not repeat it, scrub locally, warm redirect ("You don't need to tell me that here — just tell me about the memory box! 😊"), treat as a normal slip. Combined with the wall storing only canonical card text, no kid-typed PII ever reaches a file. Logging is structured-only.

Persona reconciliation (exact edit to kid-buddy.md)

One line changes (line 43, the "Get to know your buddy" paragraph). The new wording makes the gate the enforcement of "never withhold help," not its violation: the game is already built and playable, every hint gives more help, and the buddy always ends by saying the answer together. It points at the teach-and-check skill and the dial. The other persona rules (line 13 no-jargon, line 18 either/or, lines 21–22 show-don't-tell, lines 28–31 build-then-show) are already respected by design and need no edit.

Open risks / things to watch

  1. The gate is "semi," not absolute. The never-block floor means a determined kid can skip. The buddy nudges and rewards but cannot compel comprehension. PO signed off — this is the accepted consequence of the never-block requirement.
  2. Abuse disclosure where the trusted adult IS the abuser. Our posture ("tell a grown-up you trust," store nothing) is reasonable but is not a full safeguarding solution. Owner decided 2026-06-27 — ship the gentle trusted-adult handoff as-is; age-appropriate crisis resources were considered and deliberately deferred (no specifics invented). Revisit before this is in front of real kids at scale.
  3. Parent dial is a plain text file. A literate older kid can edit it. Stated honestly in the explainer rather than claiming a lock.
  4. Either/or is 50/50 guessable. The wall proves engagement + exposure, not mastery. Don't market it as proof of mastery; the optional open upgrade is the only real-recall path.
  5. LLM judge error (~1 in 4–5 even calibrated). Tuned toward GOT_IT to avoid false-NOT_YET tears. The either/or default + re-teach beat are the mitigations; the few-shot cards are the lever.
  6. In-context competence resets each session. Deliberate (no brittle state). A strong kid re-proves nothing carries over beyond the wall. Acceptable for v1.
